Haumea – The Oval-Shaped Dwarf Planet

Haumea is a dwarf planet located beyond Neptune in the Kuiper Belt. It is known for its unique elongated shape, caused by its rapid rotation, which completes a turn in less than 4 hours. Haumea is one of the fastest rotating large objects in the solar system. It has two known moons, Hiʻiaka and Namaka, and is believed to have a rocky core surrounded by a thin icy crust.

Interesting Facts, Myths, and Scientific Discoveries

  • Haumea's elongated shape is due to its rapid spin.
  • The planet has two moons, Hiʻiaka and Namaka, named after Hawaiian goddesses.
  • Haumea was discovered in 2004 and is one of the few dwarf planets officially recognized by the International Astronomical Union.
  • It has a bright icy surface, similar to Pluto and Eris.
  • Named after the Hawaiian goddess of fertility and childbirth.
