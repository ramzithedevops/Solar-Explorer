Makemake – The Mysterious Dwarf Planet

Makemake is a dwarf planet located in the Kuiper Belt, a region of the solar system beyond Neptune. It was discovered in 2005 and named after the creator god in the mythology of the Rapa Nui people of Easter Island. Makemake is known for its reddish color and the lack of an atmosphere, with temperatures dropping to -240°C (-400°F). As one of the largest known objects in the outer solar system, Makemake remains a subject of interest for astronomers and planetary scientists.

Interesting Facts and Discoveries about Makemake

  • Makemake is the third-largest known dwarf planet in the solar system, after Pluto and Eris.
  • Makemake has one known moon, which was discovered in 2016.
  • The surface of Makemake is thought to be covered with methane ice, giving it its reddish color.
  • Makemake's orbit around the Sun takes about 305 Earth years to complete.
  • The discovery of Makemake led to the redefinition of what constitutes a planet and dwarf planet in our solar system.
